Dr. Amiya Chandra

Profile

Civil Servant with wide-ranging experience in several government ministries at senior position, with comprehensive experience in governance, international trade, urban infrastructure planning, SEZs management, agriculture, policy formulation across sectors, planning and implementation.

At the time of superannuation held three charges.

  • Additional Director General of Foreign Trade, Department of Commerce, Government of India, Vanijya Bhawan, New Delhi
  • Advisor (Trade), Department of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare, Government of India, Krishi Bhawan, New Delhi
  • Development Commissioner – Adani Ports and Special Economic Zone, Mundra (Kutch), Gujarat

I have firsthand experience in students' politics. I was elected as the President of the Jawaharlal Nehru University Students' Union (1986-87) defeating the "Leftist Outfits" after many years. The experience helped in serving three Union Ministers as Private Secretary. My background in politics helped me effectively deal with the various political aspects of Governance associated with the apex political executive.

Possess specialized skills in:

  • International Trade, bilateral and multilateral affairs
  • Imports & Exports Policies
  • Agriculture production & International Trade in Agro Commodities
  • SEZ management, development and restructuring
  • Public Affairs & Public Grievances Redressal
  • Advertising & Parking Policies
  • City Management/infra-planning
  • Union Legislative / Parliamentary Affairs
  • Sports Administration / Promotion
  • Handlooms and Handicrafts

Notable Achievements

A. International Trade and Exports promotion

  • Prepared the Draft Document on "Foreign Trade Policy Vision Document, 2021-26 and "Foreign Trade Policy Vision Document 2023".
  • Instrumental in preparing presentation and recommendations on “Export Promotion with Thrust on Quality"- a theme assigned to Department of Commerce by Cabinet Secretariat.
  • As Development Commissioner of APSEZ Mundra along with additional charge of Zonal Development Commissioners Kandla SEZ, widened export's base, worked and resolved many key challenges in promotion of exports resulting in increased exports many times from SEZ's under charge.
  • Introduced many firsts in infrastructure development (instrumental in creating shared physical infrastructure for efficient utilization of energy, water, pollution control, quality control etc.) for SEZs to support existing unit holders as well as attract new investments.
  • Promoted Green SEZs concept in Gujarat, and orientation towards Zero-waste SEZs.
  • More than 3 lakh trees have been planted. New soilless and vertical farming concepts have been introduced in APSEZ and Zydus SEZ in Ahmedabad.
  • Measures like commuting by non-motorized vehicle, street furniture, way-finding signage etc. had been initiated for building a strong brand for KASEZ as well as to ensure, safety and comfort of the common visitors in the SEZ.
  • Successful in getting first Green Platinum Rating for an SEZ from CII's IGBC for environmentally sustainable development.
  • Handholding, motivating and leading the unit holders in national crisis forced by Covid-19 Pandemic and work towards providing key essential supplies to DTA like PPEs, Medical Oxygen, Oxygen cylinders, key pharmaceutical inputs and medicines etc.
  • Based on a study sponsored by Ministry of Commerce, recommendations of the study were accepted and multilateral agreement of Trade and Transit was signed between India and Central Asia Republics and Trade Route to Central Asia via Iran was operationalized.
  • Worked for 5 years in Ministry of Commerce; handling CIS and East-European Desk, and handled Debt-Repayment issue dexterously.
  • As a Lead speaker of “Niryaat – Bandhu", the flagship scheme of the DGFT for Exports Promotion, made various presentations and did hand holding (whenever required) in Universities, Export Promotion Councils, Business Schools, State Government and Ministries of the Central Government on Export Promotions in Sectors like Textiles, Agriculture, Handlooms & Handicrafts, Food Processing and Service Sector etc.

B. Agricultural Sector

  • Prepared detailed presentation “How to increase Agriculture Exports” shared during Chintan Shivir held on 7-8 July 2023 and worked on its implementation. Also submitted model for FTA negotiations on Agro commodities.
  • Weekly Inter-Ministerial coordination (IMC) for price and production monitoring of agricultural commodities.
  • Heading International desk on multilateral issues and negotiations on agricultural sector.
  • Member of IT task force for setting up of a portal related to production monitoring (Domestic and International) of Rabi, Kharif and Zaid crops.
  • Involved with Ministerial negotiations on all issues related to International Trade in agricultural sectors.

D. Urban Affairs Management

  • As Officer on Special Duty for Municipal Corporation of Delhi, managed all aspects for Outdoor Advertisement, Parking, Sanitation and other infrastructure projects on BOT basis. Street vendors and hawkers, management of factories, construction of modern slaughter house etc. Looked after the Central Zone of Delhi as Deputy Commissioner and also as Assessor and Collector of Property Tax for entire Delhi.
  • Conceptualised and implemented several infrastructure projects without adding any cost to the government, oriented towards making Delhi more habitable, clean and sanitary, accessible, gender and disable friendly. Projects like e-rickshaw, waterless urinals, high end public toilets for ladies and disable, street furniture, tehbazari policy for street vendors, G2C kiosks etc. which today have become benchmarks of city infrastructure and norms.
  • Framed the Outdoor Advertisement Policy (OAP) for Delhi in 2007, first of its kind and still the first reference point for all other state policy approach on Outdoor advertisement. The OAP was approved by the full Bench of Hon'ble Supreme Court of India, overruling its own decision of 1997 and decision of the Hon'ble High Court of Delhi.
  • Drafted an extensive Parking Policy for Delhi, with a broader time-period framework.
  • Setup operational “Guidelines for street vendors and hawkers" which is referred by many municipal corporations, implemented a simplified Unit Area Method for collection of Property Tax, operationalized door collection of garbage and generation of electricity from waste among many other initiatives
  • As PS to the Minister of Housing and Poverty Alleviation, was instrumental in drafting Street Vendors and Hawkers policy, introducing many path breaking innovations, Revised National Urban Livelihoods Mission and Rajiv Awas Yojna.

E. Indian Arts, Crafts and Culture Promotion

  • Headed premier cultural centre, "Crafts Museum" as Joint Development Commissioner- Handlooms. Headed 21 Weavers's Service Centers and 4 Indian Institutes of Handloom Technology meant for imparting skill in handloom weaving throughout India, including Varanasi, where major design interventions, loom-up gradation projects were initiated and implemented for Varanasi weavers.
  • Was instrumental in taking up various skill up-gradation initiatives for youth in consultation with National Skill Development Corporation along with driving special initiative for Punjab and North East for drug de-addiction for youth.

Key Publications

  1. Co-author of the book OOH Catalyst to SMART Urbanscape; the three-volume comprehensive book provides insights, design directions, meaningful alternatives to build SMART Urbanscape by integrating OOH media planning in city infrastructure without them being a cost to the nation.
  2. Have written two books on Central Asia: Both the books provide perspective on possible approach India can take on its ties with Central Asia and thereby improve trade and economic relationship for both regions.
    • India-Central Asia Economic Relations
    • The Trade Game: Engaging with Central Asia.
    • A book, "Indian Foreign Trade Trumped Up or Down” was written to try to demystify and understand Trump's approach to deal making through study of current events occurring within and outside of the USA and comparing them with his own past literary writings.
  3. Co-author: Natural Rock Shelters of India: Art and Painting
  4. Editor-in-Chief: ICON Journal of Archaeology and culture (an annual publication which has already published 7 volumes), which gives space to Nationalist Historians, with an attempt to rectify distortions in perspective on Indian History.
  5. Has written articles on Central Asia for leading Journals and Books.
  6. Presented papers/ delivered lectures at Rajiv Gandhi Foundation, Indian Institute of Foreign Trade, Institute of Defense Studies and Analysis and the University of Ashgabat (Turkmenistan) on affairs related to Central Asia.
